RheoAgent: A Cross-Rheological Material Handling Robotic Manipulation System via Hierarchical Decision-Making Framework

Haixu Zhang1,2,3, Bo Zhang3, Danyang Zhang1,2, Xi Chen4, Weiqiang Lai1,2, Chi Zhang5, Tin Lun Lam1,2, Hu Huang6* Yuan Gao1,2*

Abstract

Robotic manipulation in environments such as biochemical laboratories and kitchens faces challenges due to the rheological heterogeneity of materials, affecting their mechanical response to external forces. Traditional approaches often focus on manipulating materials of a single rheological type, neglecting how operational strategies must adapt when handling materials with varying flow and deformation characteristics. In this work, we introduce a hierarchical decision-making robotic manipulation system for cross-rheological material handling, namely RheoAgent, to tackle the robotics manipulation challenges posed by diverse rheological material scenarios. Our approach leverages dual-modal vision-driven reinforcement learning, which integrates pixel and voxel map representations, while additionally incorporating material classification from large vision-language models. This integration forms a system capable of flexible operations across various rheological materials. We adopt a curriculum-based differentiable training framework to balance learning efficiency and model accuracy. The policy is first pre-trained on simplified particle-based dynamics, followed by fine-tuning using a model-based short-horizon actor-critic algorithm to incorporate specific rheological characteristics. Under this framework, the pouring and gathering tasks achieved success rates of 99.08% and 95.12% for inviscid materials, 99.07% and 96.98% for viscous materials, and 98.53% and 97.51% for elasto-plastic materials, respectively. RheoAgent establishes a new framework for intelligent rheological material handling, with applications that extend to industrial automation, laboratory robotics, and assistive technologies.

Improving Material Adaptation via Differentiable MPM Fine-Tuning

To bridge the gap between simplified pre-training dynamics and complex real-world rheological behavior, we employ fine-tuning within a differentiable simulation environment based on the Material Point Method (MPM). This approach provides physically grounded gradient feedback, enabling the policy to adapt to dynamics such as yield stress, plastic flow, and contact-induced retention.

Compared to the initial pre-trained policy, the fine-tuned model exhibits more stable interactions and improved task success under non-ideal conditions, such as deformable surface adherence or delayed flow onset. Notably, fine-tuning enhances the agent’s ability to modulate tool velocity, angle, and path in response to subtle rheological cues—capabilities that are critical for handling viscous and elasto-plastic materials.

Real-time Segmentation and Perception Mapping

We employ the Segment Anything Model 2 (SAM2) to perform real-time segmentation of rheological materials from RGB-D inputs. To build a consistent spatial understanding, multi-view RGB-D frames are fused into a globally aligned segmented point cloud. This point cloud is then encoded into two representations:

  • Pixel map: generated via orthographic projection of the segmented point cloud onto the vertical plane.
  • Voxel map: constructed by encoding spatial occupancy on a latitude-longitude grid with normalized distances.

These representations serve as structured perceptual inputs for downstream robotic manipulation policies.

This representation captures two fundamental characteristics of rheological materials—fluidity and deformability. This setup demonstrates cross-embodiment task decomposition and cooperation under shared perception.

Material Properties Reasoning

To enable autonomous manipulation of unknown rheological materials, RheoAgent integrates visual and physical reasoning. Material type is first identified using GPT-4V based on RGB inputs, distinguishing between granular, fluid, and elasto-plastic categories. For non-granular materials, the robot estimates density via force sensing and viscosity via standardized shaking, with GPT-4V comparing dynamic responses to known references. These physical properties guide targeted fine-tuning in a differentiable simulation environment. Segmented point clouds from multi-view RGB-D inputs are encoded into pixel and voxel maps, serving as structured observations for downstream policy adaptation.

Pipeline for identifying material rheological properties. This comprises material type classification via visual perception, density estimation through robotic interaction, and viscosity estimation via dynamic response analysis.

How Does It Work?

Our framework, RheoAgent, integrates hierarchical reinforcement learning with differentiable simulation and dual-view visual encoding. It begins with model-free pre-training in a Unity environment using egocentric voxel maps, allocentric pixel maps, and proprioceptive vectors as inputs. The policy is guided by a reward function combining distribution distance and density discrepancy terms. Fine-tuning is performed in FluidLab using differentiable reward gradients for sample-efficient optimization. In the real world, RGB-D data from depth sensors are segmented by SAM2, then encoded into voxel and pixel maps for policy deployment. This pipeline enables robust generalization across materials and tasks by aligning visual representations between simulation and reality.
